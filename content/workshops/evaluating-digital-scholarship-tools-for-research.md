# Evaluating Digital Scholarship Tools for Research

“Research practices are shaped by the digital tools we adopt. Thoughtful evaluation of technologies and methods can align digital scholarship with research goals, values, and impact ambitions.”

## Short Description
Digital tools shape how research is created, analyzed, and shared. This workshop helps researchers critically evaluate digital scholarship techniques and technologies — from data tools to interactive platforms — and select approaches that align with their research goals, ethics, and impact ambitions.

## Relevance to Impact
This workshop addresses a growing challenge in research practice: digital tools are often adopted without clear alignment to research goals, values, or impact intentions. By building critical evaluation skills and digital literacy, it helps researchers make more intentional choices about technologies and methods. This supports responsible, ethical digital scholarship and strengthens the ability of research outputs to be accessible, sustainable, and meaningful beyond academic contexts.

## Who It’s For
Researchers exploring or using digital humanities or digital scholarship methods, including those building new workflows or evaluating technological options for their projects.

## What You’ll Learn / Outcomes
- Understand digital humanities approaches and how they align with research questions  
- Improve digital and data literacy for choosing appropriate tools  
- Evaluate technology options using clear, practical criteria  
- Build practices for selecting and integrating digital methods into your research process  

## Format
Interactive workshop (90 minutes)  
In-person at the Library  
Group size: 10–15 participants  
On-demand scheduling  

## What to Bring
Nothing required; exercises and examples will be provided.

## Materials Provided
Guides, evaluation criteria templates, examples of digital scholarship tools, and links to resources.

## Pathways Alignment
- Skill Development  
- Knowledge Sharing  
- Impact Strategy & Planning  
- Open Scholarship & Responsible Use of Technology
